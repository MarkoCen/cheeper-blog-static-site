Is There a PlayStation 5 (PS5)? Updates on Specs and Release

02/12/2017
Previews
Game, PlayStation, Sony

PS4 Pro just out for couple of months and we are already hearing rumors on Sony next generation console - PlayStation 5. Up to date, there is no official announcement about PS5’s release and specs, but there is one thing we could expect that its power would be formidable compared to other console systems. Who knows, it might surpass Microsoft’s Project Scorpio as well.

VR compatibility with 4K resolution

Sony released PlayStation VR and became the first console manufacturer embracing VR technology, of course, PlayStation VR is competing with the Oculus Rift and the HTC Vive, and it’s cheaper with less resolution and frame rates, but the frame rates would be improved if it running on a PS4 Pro. This proved that Sony has jumped on board of virtual reality. So it’s a good bet that, Sony would like to return to the VR market with a higher-end VR equipment(maybe called PlayStation VR 2?) with PS5, and we have enough reasons to believe PS5 would take VR gaming experience to a next level.

We all know VR is the future, but 4k is the present. No matter Xbox One S or PlayStation 4 Pro, both are 4K compatible, and PS5 would support playing games in native 4K. Mark Cerny is the lead architect on the PS4 and the PS4 Pro, in an interview with the Japanese site AV Watch, Cerny was talking about the PS4 Pro and the argument surrounding 4K, he said that the image reaches resolutions are “extremely close to 4K”, this leads us to believe that the new system will most certainly run games at 4K. Mark’s comments also give us a potential window into the PS5, he believes that 4K is only achievable with 8 teraflops of power, then it’s safe to assume he’s going to shoot for this when he’s designing the PS5.

A Cloud-Based Gaming Platform?

Recently, Masayasu Ito, Sony executive vice president, shared his thoughts on the PS5 with the online edition of the Japanese publication Nikkei:

I think there will be a PS5. However, I don’t know what form it’ll have. It could be a physical console, or it could be in the cloud. But even if the form might change, PS5 Games will be born. This is what we want.

This year, Sony has made the first step to cloud gaming services with the release of PlayStation Now and the PlayStation TV console. However, all exsiting services haven’t exactly taken off, Sony likes the idea of a streaming game service, but mostly the gradual introduction of new hardware like PlayStation VR and new services like PlayStation Now gives them the opportunity to test the waters. Once Sony has all the results, they would make a final desicion on PS5’s form.

Dualshock 5 Controller

There is another exciting concept - DualShock 5 Controller, people guessing Dualshock 5 will be on the ultimate ergonomic controller experience, the touchpad is switched out for a touchscreen which could able to display everything from radar, to health, to other creative concepts. Also wireless charging would be intergrated with PS5 controller, with wireless charging, gamers can quickly and easily recharge the controller when it gets low. Additional features we can expect would be inspired by Xbox controllers that have a lot of customization options. For example, the sensitivity of the buttons will be adjustable in PS5 apps.

Releasing in 2018?

In an interview with The Verge, Shawn Layden, head of SIEA and the PlayStation Worldwide Studios Group said:

Right now we were concentrating on how do we iterate within this PS4 life cycle. The technology and improvements behind PS4 Plus are our way of articulating where we think the market wants to go. People will want to have greater fidelity of images and graphics. Where we go from there, we’re going to have to wait and see. It’s our first time innovating within the life cycle, so I’m not exactly sure what impact that will have on our plan going forward. But there will be more PlayStations.

Meanwhile, Andrew House, the CEO of Sony Interactive Entertainment said in E3 2016:

I’m not suggesting we want to bring the games industry to an 18-month-two-year cycle because then you would lose an awful lot of the fixed platform benefits we’ve enjoyed that allow for these really great leaps in game experience.

However, we did think there was an opportunity to reflect on the traditional lifecycle, and on 4K technology, and say maybe there’s an opportunity, within the course of a normal lifecycle to offer something else. Something a little bit better, for a segment of the market that feels that this is important.

Numerous rumors said PS5 would be announced on 2018, one of the famous prediction came from Damian Thong who accurately predicting the PS4 Pro and Slim released:

PS4 Pro? Wait, what about PS5? Macquarie's Damian Thong who correctly predicted PS4 slim and Pro says PS5 may arrive in 2nd half of 2018.

— Takashi Mochizuki (@mochi_wsj) September 8, 2016

PS4 Pro released in 2016, and based on above comments, it’s fair to say we won’t see the PS5 in 2017, but we can expect PS5 will be the generational leap console we’re looking for
